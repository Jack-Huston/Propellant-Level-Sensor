LOX/RP-1 Propellant Level Sensor

A cryo-capacitance liquid level sensor built for CU Boulder’s Sounding Rocket Lab (SRL) test stand.

2021 – 2022
CU Boulder • SRL
Solo Project

Project Overview

Prototype concentric-rod capacitance sensor assembly

Mission

Design and build a robust liquid level sensor to measure LOX and RP-1 levels in SRL’s engine test stand tanks. The instrument needed to survive high-pressure plumbing and cryogenic temperatures while providing a simple, reliable output for stand operations.

Challenge

Create a capacitance-based measurement that works across fluids with very different permittivities, functions in cryogenic and ambient water test conditions, and fits within SRL’s existing DAQ/control toolchain without adding system complexity.

Solution

Implemented a concentric two-rod stainless-steel sensor with PTFE end caps for electrical isolation and pressure sealing. Used an Arduino Uno’s known internal trace capacitance to form a capacitor divider with the sensor, enabling capacitance-to-height conversion and a filtered 0–5 V analog output proportional to fill level.

Design & Development

Capacitive Sensing Principle

Two concentric stainless rods form a capacitor. As liquid fills the annulus, the effective permittivity increases and the capacitance rises. This shift is mapped to liquid height using the fluid’s properties and the sensor’s geometry.

  • Stainless concentric electrodes
  • PTFE end caps provide isolation and pressure passthrough
  • Capacitance range: ~36–58 pF in normal operation
  • Water testing observed ~2142 pF (high εr)
Concentric-rod geometry and fluid fill region

Electronics & Readout

The Arduino’s known internal trace capacitance forms a divider with the sensor. Applying a step yields a proportional voltage that is converted to capacitance, then to fluid height. Height is output as a 0–5 V analog level via PWM filtered by a low-pass network.

  • Internal capacitance ≈ 30 pF (characterized for accuracy)
  • Resolution: ~1% for 3.5–225 pF; ~5% up to 2 nF
  • Minimum height resolution ≈ ±11.5 mm (from method error)
  • Optional parallel cap (~1250 pF) extends range for water tests
Arduino divider readout and RC smoothing for analog output

Mechanical & Integration

The stainless/PTFE assembly was designed for high-pressure plumbing and cryogenic exposure. The output voltage integrates directly with SRL test-stand DAQ for real-time level visualization and control logic.

  • Stainless rods and tube hardware for durability
  • PTFE dielectric and seals for isolation
  • Bench and water-flow calibration prior to stand integration

Key Achievements

Robust Cryo-Capacitive Design

Concentric stainless rods with PTFE isolation survived handling and plumbing for test-stand integration.

Direct DAQ Integration

Generated a filtered 0–5 V analog signal mapping 0–100% fill, simplifying stand operations.

Range Extensible for Water Tests

Optional ~1250 pF parallel capacitor extended measurable range for high-εr water flow testing.

Lessons Learned

Characterizing the Arduino’s internal capacitance is critical for accuracy. Fluid permittivity changes and temperature effects drive the need for fluid-specific calibration. RC filter selection must balance output smoothness with response time for fill-rate transients.

Future Work

Add temperature compensation, implement a dedicated capacitance-to-digital converter for higher resolution, and perform full cryogenic calibration with LOX and RP-1 to refine height mapping across operating conditions.
